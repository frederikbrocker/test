Concept 1 — Portfolio Monitoring

01 — Executive summary
Nordic PE funds spend days every month chasing CFOs by email, cleaning Excel, and consolidating data that arrives weeks late. The accounting data already exists in e-conomic. Portfolio Monitoring pulls it directly to the fund — live dashboard, automatic alerts, one-click LP reports.
"From monthly fire drill to always-on portfolio intelligence."
🟢
Desirability
Pain is real and confirmed. Investment Managers spend multiple days every month on manual data collection — chasing, formatting and consolidating. Extracting insights from the reports takes hours each month.
🔴
Viability
The pain is high-frequency and recurring, but the addressable market is structurally small — Nordic PE funds are few and individual fund sizes limit seat count. Build and operational complexity is likely to outweigh the commercial potential at current market size.
🔴
Feasibility
Fragmented ERP landscape — most portcos don't run e-conomic, so our data advantage is thin. Bookkeeping isn't truly live; delayed entries make real-time signals unreliable. International group structures add consolidation complexity we haven't solved for.
From research interviews (13 interviews)
"It's worth a lot of money if you can pull it off. But we just haven't found anyone who could do it well and efficiently."
Marius · Nordic Alpha Partners
"A standardised reporting flow from portfolio companies into a live KPI view — that would replace all the Excel-based quarterly reporting we do today."
Kim · Monterro
"What you need to convince someone like us is that you can bind it together end-to-end — from source data all the way through to investor reporting and LP reporting. Fully agnostic across accounting systems, ERP systems, and CRM systems."
Marius · Nordic Alpha Partners
Critical assumptions
#Assumption to validate
1 ⚠E-conomic penetration is high enough to make the product viable
The addressable market depends entirely on how many portcos in target funds actually use e-conomic.
2 ⚠E-conomic data alone is sufficient to create value
Additional ERP integrations or manual uploads would significantly increase complexity and reduce the unfair advantage.
3 ⚠Willingness to pay justifies the build
Fund managers pay enough to cover development and operational costs at the expected market size.
4 ⚠The portfolio company could be a different ICP — changing the perspective on the concept entirely
We already have a relation with portcos through e-conomic. If they are the buyer — not the fund — it may open a different and potentially more viable angle on the product.
🛑
Recommendation
We do not recommend proceeding. Low viability and feasibility — the ecosystem is too fragmented, and expected e-conomic penetration across portfolios is too low to build a commercially viable product. Complexity outweighs the opportunity.
02 — The problem
📋 Data arrives late and inconsistent
Every CFO formats differently. Numbers arrive 2–4 weeks late, often incomplete. No standardised, comparable view across the portfolio exists.
🔍 FTE time spent chasing and extracting, not deciding
Days each month go to chasing missing data — and even when it arrives, insights still require manual analysis to surface. The investment manager is a data processor, not a decision maker.
📤 External reporting is built by hand every time
LP updates, board packs, quarterly reviews — all assembled manually, every cycle. Days of effort for data that should already be structured.

Concept 2 — Market Intelligence

01 — Executive summary
Nordic M&A teams spend up to 14 days per sprint manually screening companies on 12–18 month old filings — before they even know if a target is worth pursuing. The live financials already exist in e-conomic. Market Intelligence makes them searchable: screen 300k+ companies instantly with real numbers, and generate an investment-ready data pack in one click.
"From weeks of manual screening to a same-day, data-driven longlist."
🟢
Desirability
Screening is time consuming — analysts lose 1–2 days per target before knowing if it's worth pursuing. Data is outdated — filings are 12–18 months old by the time they're read. And slow qualification means real risk: competitors who move faster win the deal.
🟡
Viability
Strong willingness to pay — established tools validate deal teams pay real money for data, though existing solutions rely on public data only. WTP validated for a Danish starting point. Reaching Nordic scale across multiple customer segments is the growth lever needed to make this a relevant Growth Lab case.
🟡
Feasibility
Technically buildable — public data (CVR + annual reports) as the foundation, e-conomic as the live differentiating layer. Key blockers sit in data anonymisation, legal authorisation, and opt-in model design. Requires mothership investment in data management to proceed.
From research interviews (13 interviews)
"It's ticket to play — you have to pay for it. But is it too expensive? Absolutely. The problem is there are no alternatives."
Thomas · Axcel
"The more data the better. We're lucky there's transparency in public data, but it has its limits."
Josefine C.W. Obel
"A buying decision is a risk/reward calculation — the more data that can de-risk it, the more it's worth."
Kim · Monterro
Critical assumptions
#Assumption to validate
1 ⚠Anonymisation preserves value
Anonymised, aggregated data retains enough business value to be worth paying for.
2 ⚠Tech can guarantee anonymisation
We can robustly anonymise data at scale, and revenue potential justifies the data management investment.
3 ⚠Brand risk is outweighed by value
Data sharing creates enough value for all parties that it doesn't damage our core brand.
4 ⚠Vendor access can be controlled
We can technically and legally ensure only approved vendors access the data.
5 ⚠The business model drives opt-in
The commercial model incentivises both data sellers and buyers to participate.
✅
Recommendation
We recommend proceeding to incubation. The pain is validated, the data moat is structural, and early research shows strong demand. The critical assumptions around data, legal, and business model design are the work of incubation — not blockers to starting.
02 — The problem
🕐 Screening is time consuming and manual
Analysts spend up to 14 days per sprint manually working through company lists — qualifying targets one by one before knowing if any are worth pursuing.
📊 Data is outdated and limited to public availability
Annual filings are 12–18 months old by the time they're read. Existing tools rely on public data only — live financials for private companies simply don't exist anywhere.
📈 No contextual awareness in the market
Is a target performing well — or just riding a rising market? Without live peer benchmarks, there's no way to know if you've found a standout or just the average.
