Competitive landscape

White space — one defensible sentence
"Every PE portfolio monitoring tool on the market today still relies on the PortCo CFO sending in templated data. We're the only one that pulls live data directly from the SMB ERPs the PortCos actually use — because we own one of those ERPs."
⚠ The real competitor
Excel + email. Most lower-mid-market Nordic funds use manual templates and email chasing today. You're not displacing iLEVEL — you're displacing free-but-painful workflows. Nordic Alpha tried Tableau + Alteryx and abandoned it: "It just grew too big. We'd have ended up with huge consultant costs." That failure is your structural wedge.

Competitive landscape

White space — one defensible sentence
"Capital IQ, PitchBook and Mergermarket all default to listed-company proxies when you search for a Nordic €15M private SaaS company — because that's all they have. We give you actual private peer financials updated monthly, in a pitch-ready format, for less than Mergermarket's seat price."
⚠ The real competitor
A junior analyst manually scraping CVR + annual reports + LinkedIn. That's the actual status quo at most boutique M&A firms today. You're not displacing Mergermarket — you're displacing free-but-painful junior analyst time. Easier sell, but a lower price ceiling to navigate.
